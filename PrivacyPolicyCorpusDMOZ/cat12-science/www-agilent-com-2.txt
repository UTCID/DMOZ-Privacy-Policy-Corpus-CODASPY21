Types of Data Collected Through Cookies and Purposes of Cookies’ Use
Agilent uses technologies, such as web-tracking tools designed for website session analysis and profiling of the users of our Website. Such technologies enable us to optimize our Website content and tailor it and other communications to you.
A cookie is a text file containing small amounts of information stored on your computer’s hard drive when you visit a webpage. Session cookies are erased when the user closes the web browser or browser tab. Persistent cookies remain on your hard drive until they expire or are deleted. Cookies will also be set by third parties operating on our behalf (e.g. to help us analyze web traffic or to improve your web navigation experience).
Some cookies are necessary for our website’s functionality and enable you to place an item in our online cart and to place an order on our website. Other cookies allow us to enhance and personalize your Agilent.com web browsing experience, so the information you receive is more relevant to you. Here are some examples of what cookies allow us to do:
Remember what items are in your online cart;
Recognize when you return to our website;
Enable faster checkout and remember what you ordered if you have created an account on Agilent.com;
Analyze how our customers navigate our website and which products they search for; and
Deliver personalized marketing from Agilent.
If you do not want information collected through the use of cookies, most browsers allow you to automatically decline cookies or be given the choice of declining or accepting a particular cookie (or cookies) from a particular website. You can find more information about cookies and how to manage them at http://www.allaboutcookies.org/. If you set your browser to refuse all cookies, please contact us directly to place your order.
