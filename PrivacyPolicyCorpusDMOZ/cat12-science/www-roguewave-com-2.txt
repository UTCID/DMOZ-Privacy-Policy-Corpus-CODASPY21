Information we may collect about you and how we collect it
We collect two kinds of information, personal information that can be used to identify you, and non-personal information.
In general, you can visit this website without telling us who you are or revealing any personal information. There are times, however, when we may gather information from you that you provide voluntarily. The types of personal information we collect about you that can be used to identify you as an individual person including things like your name, address, email address, country of residence, the company you work for, and phone number.
We also collect non-personal information when you use our website, such as information about your internet connection, the equipment you use to access our website, and usage details, and search queries submitted on our website.
We collect personal information directly from you when you provide it to us by, for example, filling out the forms on our website, communicating with us via email, or submitting search queries on the website.
We also may collect non-personal information automatically as you navigate through the site through the use of technology such as cookies, flash cookies, and web beacons. Using this kind of technology, our web servers automatically recognize and collect the domain name and IP address of visitors to our website. This kind of technology may transfer data to your browser that can be read by us as a sort of a non-personal identification card, recording information about your past visits to our website. It also can be combined with personal information. In addition, we collect aggregate information on web page visits.
We do not respond to "do-not-track" signals or other mechanisms that allow users to signal their preferences on the collection of their personal information over time and across third-party websites or online services. This website does not monitor for or adjust any use as the result of "do-not-track" signals.
