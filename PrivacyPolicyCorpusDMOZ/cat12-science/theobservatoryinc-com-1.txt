days. There are about 365.24 days in a year. Therefore, about 12.37 lunations
(365.24 days divided by 29.53 days) happen in a tropical year. In the broadly
used Gregorian calendar, there are 12 months (the word month derived from moon)
in a year, and usually there is one full moon every month.
Each calendar year contains approximately 11
days more than the number of days in 12 lunar cycles. The additional days
accumulate, so every two or three years (seven times in the 19-year Metonic
cycle), there is an additional full moon. The additional full moon essentially
falls in one of the four seasons, giving that season for full moons rather than
standard three, and, hence, a blue moon....
