Privacy Statement
curiouser.co.uk does not share any information about you with any other parties.
We do not collect personal information such as names, addresses etc. However, we may
collect some general information for our own statistical analysis in order to
improve the site, such as type of browser, country, server etc. We do store addresses
of those people who choose to email us, but this information is only used to occasionally
distribute relevant information pertaining to this site.
This web site contains links to other sites. Viewers
should note that we are not responsible for the privacy policies of
any of these sites. Viewers are encouraged to read the privacy statements
of any web sites they may be directed to from curiouser.co.uk.
Cookies
To make this site work properly, we sometimes place small data files called cookies on your device. Most big websites do this too.
What are cookies?
A cookie is a small text file that a website saves on your computer or mobile device when you visit the site. It enables the website to remember your actions and preferences (such as login, language, font size and other display preferences) over a period of time, so you don’t have to keep re-entering them whenever you come back to the site or browse from one page to another.
How do we use cookies?
Third-party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on users' prior visits to this site.
This site uses Google Analytics to gather anonymous statistics about site traffic and Google Adsense to provide advertising.
For more information about the cookies associated with these products please visit the following page:
How Google uses data when you use our partners' sites or apps.
How to control cookies
You can control and/or delete cookies as you wish. For details, see aboutcookies.org. You can delete all cookies that are already on your computer and you can set most browsers to prevent them from being placed. If you do this, however, you may have to manually adjust some preferences every time you visit a site and some services and functionalities may not work.
Google uses the DART cookie which enables it and its partners to serve ads based visitors viewing of this site and/or other sites on the Internet.
Visitors may opt out of the use of the DART cookie by visiting google's advertising opt-out page.
curiouser.co.uk enables third-party vendors and ad networks to serve adverts on this site through google (see list of vendors that are certified for third-party ads by google.
Visitors may visit those websites to opt out of cookies (if the vendor or ad network offers this capability). Visitors can opt out of some, but not all, of these cookies in one location at the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out
website at http://www.networkadvertising.org/managing/opt_out.asp.
