Information Collection
We collect Personal Information about you.  This Personal Information may include:

Your IP address.

Metadata from your communications with us.
IP Addresses.  An IP address is a number assigned to your computer when you connect to the Internet.  As part of the protocol of the Internet, web servers can identify your computer by its IP address.  In addition, web servers may be able to identify the type of browser and/or type of computer you are using.  We collect IP addresses and related information for the purposes of system administration, to assess the traffic to the Site, and to maintain and improve the Site.  For security purposes, we may link account information you enter when you purchase products on the Site to the originating IP address, but we do not otherwise link IP addresses to your personally identifiable information.  We can and will use IP addresses and other information we possess to identify a user when we feel it is necessary to enforce compliance with this Privacy Policy or our Terms of Use, to protect our service, the Site, customers or others, or to comply with applicable laws. Cookies.  Cookies are small bits of information that some websites (including the Site) transfer to an individual's hard drive.  We use cookies to improve users’ experience on our Site, for example to personalize features of the Site and for you, and to analyze the traffic, such as total visitors and pages viewed on the Site, and make improvements based on the data we collect.  We never save passwords or credit card information in cookies.  The use of cookies has become a standard in the industry.  Most browsers are initially set up to accept cookies.  You can usually set your browser to refuse cookies or indicate when a cookie is being sent.  If you refuse cookies, you may not be able to use some of the functionality of the Site.   We use cookies to personalize content and ads, provide social media recommendations and analyze website traffic. We also share information about how you use our site with our social media, advertising and analytics partners who may combine it with other information you have provided to them or that they’ve collected from your use of their services. Review our full cookie policy here. Web Beacons. Web beacons are small bits of code that communicate with third parties and are embedded in web pages, our ads or in emails. We may use web beacons to deliver or communicate with cookies, to count users who have visited a web page, to understand usage patterns or other third party functionality. We also may include web beacons in e-mails to learn if messages have been opened, acted on, or forwarded. You can disable the ability of Web beacons to store cookie information by declining cookies. Do Not Track.  As technologies to address Do Not Track signals are still evolving, we do not currently deploy any technology to respond to Do Not Track signals. Browser plug-ins/add-ons.  Additional web components that may need to be installed to enable certain web features on our Site. You have the option not to install these components.
