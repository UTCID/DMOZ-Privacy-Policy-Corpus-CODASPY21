Cookie policy
Our website uses cookies to give you the best online experience.  A message bar will appear in your device’s browser when you first visit our site, or every time you visit if you have not accepted our cookies. If your browser is set not to accept cookies, then you may not be able to use some of our website’s features.
What is a cookie?
A cookie is a small text file that is stored on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used in order to make websites work effectively and efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site.
Cookies in emails
In addition to the cookies we use on this website, we also use cookies and similar technologies in emails. These help us to understand whether you have opened the email and how you have engaged with it; knowing which articles are most popular helps us to improve and optimise our content for the future. Cookies will also be set if you click on any link within the email.
How we use cookies
Our website uses cookies in the following ways based on the International Chamber of Commerce guide for cookie categories:
Strictly necessary
These cookies are essential for the operation of our website.  Without these cookies you would be unable to shop for products on our website or log in.  These cookies do not gather information about you that could be used for marketing purposes.  This category of cookies cannot be disabled.
Functionality
These cookies allow our website to remember choices (preferences) you have made, and help us provide you with a better, more personal experience. For instance, if you have already completed a form, we will remember your preferences so that you do not have to complete it again.
Performance
These cookies are used to collect information about how visitors use our site, whether as a new or returning visitor, which pages are visited most often and if visitors have been referred from another site (e.g. a search engine). This information is used to help us improve our website, and ensure visitors are finding what they are looking for easily.
Targeting
We also use cookies to track the effectiveness of our marketing communications, for example by telling us if you have opened a marketing email that we have sent you, viewed a blog or campaign landing page.  We may also share this information with third parties such as advertising partners to serve you with advertisements elsewhere on the internet.

Managing cookies
Most web browsers automatically accept cookies as the default setting, but you can usually modify your browser settings to accept, delete, or decline cookies if you prefer. If you choose to decline cookies, you may not be able to fully experience the features of the website. For example, when shopping for products.
To find out more about cookies, including how to see what cookies have been set and how to manage and delete them, visit www.aboutcookies.org or www.allaboutcookies.org.
