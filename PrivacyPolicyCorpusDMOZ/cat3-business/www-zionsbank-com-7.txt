Thinking About Switching Banks?
We’re Here to Make the Switch Quick and Easy
Zions Bank has been a leading community bank in Utah and Idaho since 1873. We’re committed to provide the best service and products that fit your lifestyle. Whether that’s doing it all digitally with Online and Mobile Banking or visiting with our friendly, knowledgeable employees at your local branch. We’re here to make the switch quick and easy!
Step 1: Open A Checking Account at Zions Bank
Online at the button below and choose an account.
Or visit your local Zions Bank branch
Open Account
Included in your new checking account:
Debit card
Digital Banking
Bill Pay
eDocuments
Step 2: Sign Up For Digital Banking
Online at: www.zionsbank.com/digital-banking
Select Online Banking and then click ‘Enroll Now’
Enroll Now
The Direct Deposit form can be found on your Online Banking
Login to Online Banking
Select the services tab
In the section labeled Account Services, click on 'Direct Deposit Request'
Step 3: Transfer Direct Deposits
Contact companies that you’ve setup automated payments with such as your insurance company, gym or local utilities.
The Direct Deposit form can be found within Online Banking.
Login to Online banking
Select the Services tab
In the section labeled Account Services, click on 'Direct Deposit Request'
Step 4: Transfer Automatic Payments
Online at:  www.zionsbank.com/digital-banking
Select Online Banking and then click ‘Enroll Now’
Have your new Zions Bank account number on hand along with the Zions Bank routing number.
Zions Bank Routing Number: 124000054
Step 5: Close Your Old Account
Let’s take a minute and make sure you’re ready to take this step:
Have you received your Zions Bank debit card and PIN?
Have you enrolled in Online Banking and Bill Pay?
Have you downloaded the Zions Bank Mobile App?
Have you switched all your Direct Deposit and Automatic Payments?
Have all your transactions (checks, debit card purchases, transfers, automatic payments, etc.) cleared your old account?
If the answer is yes to these questions, then it’s time to take the final step and close out that old account. Simply contact your former Financial Institution, let them know you want to close out your account, and follow their processes.
