This privacy policy describes the information Tesia Clearinghouse, LLC collects about you and how it is used or shared, what rights and choices you have over your personal information, and measures we take to protect your information. This privacy policy applies to personal information collected by or on behalf of Tesia Clearinghouse, LLC (“Tesia,” “we” or “us”) through our website https://tesia.com website and mobile versions of our website (collectively, the “Site”), by phone or other offline contacts and anywhere else we display this privacy policy. By using the Site, you accept the terms of this privacy policy in effect at the time of your visit.
Personal Information We Collect from the Site
Personal information is information directly or indirectly identifies you or can be used to identify you as an individual. The following are the categories of information we collect through the Site and how we collect it:
• Information you provide. You may provide personal information to us when you use the Site. For example, you may provide your contact information such as your name, e-mail address, or telephone number when you contact us through the Site. You can choose not to give us your personal information but in some cases, we will not be able to respond to your request or answer your questions without certain contact information.
• Information collected automatically. We may automatically collect certain information about you or your device when you visit or interact with the Site using tools such as pixels, log files or “cookies.” Cookies are small data files stored on your browser when you visit the Site that collect information about your use of the Site to help us improve your access to the Site and the services we provide. This information typically remains in aggregate form and does not identify individual users. If a combination of information we collect does identify you as individual, we will treat the combined information as personal information. The information we collect using pixels, log files and cookies may include information such as IP address, device ID, browser type and other information associated with your device or the pages you interact with on the Site. We use this information in aggregate form to enhance the experience and operation of the Site, analyze trends such as Site usage, to administer and secure the Site, and to evaluate the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns on other platforms. You can adjust your browser settings to accept or reject cookies; however, if you disable all cookies, you may not be able to access all features of the Site. We do not permit third parties to track your online activities over time and across other websites based on your use of the Site.
The Site also uses analytics tools such as Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google, Inc. that uses cookies to help the Site analyze how users navigate the Site. The information generated by the cookie about your use of the Site (including your IP address) will be transmitted to and stored by Google in aggregate form. Google uses this aggregated information on our behalf in order to evaluate use of the Site, compile reports on Site activity and other services relating to Site usage. You can learn more about Google Analytics and how to opt out here: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.

We use your personal information to accomplish our business and commercial purposes, including to:

You can opt out of email marketing communications from us at any time by using the unsubscribe link in our emails or you can email us at info@tesia.com.

• Comply with our legal obligations or to respond to law enforcement or legal or regulatory inquires or requests or legal proceedings.
• Protect our legal rights, your health and safety or the health and safety of our employees or others.
When We Share Your Information
Except as described in this privacy policy, we do not sell, share or rent your personal information without your consent. We may share your personal information with the following categories of recipients:

• Other third parties as necessary to protect your safety or the safety of others, to protect our legal interests or to comply with applicable laws and regulations or our legal requirements.

However, information transmitted over the Internet cannot be 100% secured and we do not guarantee your information will be secure from theft, loss or unauthorized access or use.

Some browsers have “do-not-track” features that allow you to tell a website not to track you. These features are not all uniform. The Site does not currently respond to browser-based “do-not-track” signals.
