All data subjects have a right of access in accordance with Art. 15 GDPR, a right to rectification in accordance with Art. 16 GDPR, a right to erasure in accordance with Art. 17 GDPR, a right to restriction of processing in accordance with Art. 18 GDPR, a right to object in accordance with Art. 21 GDPR and a right to data portability in accordance with Art. 20 GDPR.
The right of access and the right to erasure are subject to the restrictions provided for in Articles 34 and 35 BDSG.
You may withdraw your consent to the processing of your personal data at any time. This also applies to declarations of consent given before the General Data Protection Regulation came into force, i.e. before May 25, 2018. Please note that the withdrawal of consent is only effective for the future and does not affect data processing which has taken place before we receive the withdrawal of your consent.
Furthermore, you have a right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority (Art. 77 GDPR in conjunction with Article 19 BDSG). You will find a list of supervisory authorities (for the non-public sector) together with their addresses at
