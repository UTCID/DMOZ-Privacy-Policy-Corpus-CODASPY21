Browser Cookies And Web Beacons
We may use browser "cookies” or web beacons to make our sites and services more useful to you. Browser cookies are small data files placed on the computer hard drives of users of our web and mobile apps.   Cookies help us to tailor our apps to be more helpful and efficient for you. The cookie consists of a unique identifier that does not contain information about you or your health history. We use two types of cookies:
Session cookies help customize your experience on our site, by maintaining your signed-on status as you navigate through, and tracking your "click path” through our pages or screens. Session cookies are temporary, and expire after the session ends and you close your browser or app.
Persistent cookies remain on your hard drive after you've exited from our website. If you've given us permission to send you information about our services, we may place a persistent cookie on your hard drive that will let us know when you come back to visit our site.
We may also use cookies in combination with a "Web beacon". Web beacons are small graphic files that are also used to understand how you are using the site.

You may opt-in or opt-out of sharing location data from your computer by through the settings on your browser or mobile device.
You may have software on your computer that will allow you to decline or deactivate cookies and beacons. If you do so, some features of the Websites or the App may not work properly for you. For instructions on how to remove cookies from your hard drive, go to your browser's website for detailed instructions.
