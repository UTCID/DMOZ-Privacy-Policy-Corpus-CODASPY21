What are Cookies?
Cookies are small encrypted text files that are placed on your computer or mobile phone when you browse websites. Cookies allow a website to recognise your device and help your browser navigate through the website. They can allow you to log in automatically or remember settings you selected during earlier visits (to name a few). Cookies don’t harm your computer. If you want to learn more about cookies, visit www.allaboutcookies.org
2. Use of cookies on this website
Cookies help us to make your website work as you’d expect and provide you with a good experience. We do not use cookies to collect or record information like your name, address or payment details. Sometimes we link information from cookies with you, like when you are using our website and make an enquiry. In such cases, the combined data is your personal information and you should also read our privacy policy.
3. Why we use cookies
We use cookies on this website for the for the following purposes:
Strictly Necessary Cookies – these cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be turned off in our systems.
Measurement and Analytics Cookies – these cookies collect anonymised statistical information about how visitors use this website and analyse behaviours. We need these cookies to continue improving how the website works and measure the success of campaigns.
Marketing Cookies – these cookies ensure that the marketing content you are served on other websites is MYA’s  and relevant to your interests. Please be aware, if you do not consent to Marketing Cookies, you may still be served prospecting adverts.
