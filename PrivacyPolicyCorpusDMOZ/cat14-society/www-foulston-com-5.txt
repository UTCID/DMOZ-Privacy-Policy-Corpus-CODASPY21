Privacy Policy
These policies are applicable to www.foulston.com and all other public sites on the World Wide Web operated by Foulston Siefkin LLP.
Foulston Siefkin LLP does not currently collect personal identifying information ("Information") except 1) to the extent that your web browser provides Information to our web server (such as the site you linked from, the identity of your Internet Service Provider, or the type of browser you are using); 2) to the extent that you provide Information to us in an e-mail message; and 3) to the extent that you provide Information to us in a web page form (for example, when you complete a feedback form). Foulston Siefkin LLP will use your Information only for that specific purpose for which you submitted the Information, except that Foulston Siefkin LLP may use Information it obtains from its site in an aggregated form in order to gauge the usefulness and popularity of its web pages, or links to or from our site.
Cookies are pieces of information used for recordkeeping that some web sites store on the computer that is browsing that web site ("Cookies"). Foulston Siefkin does not currently use Cookies on its web site; however it may decide to use them in the future. If you have your browser set to not accept Cookies, you may experience slightly slower response times.
Foulston Siefkin LLP reserves the right to change this policy at any time by posting a new privacy policy at this location.
