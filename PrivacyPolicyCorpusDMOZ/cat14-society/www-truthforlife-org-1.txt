Donor Privacy Policy
Truth For Life takes the privacy of our donors very seriously. Any information that is shared with us is treated confidentially. We never sell  or trade the names of any donors or listeners to other organizations, nor do we rent our mailing list to other organizations. No mailings will be sent on behalf of other organizations. All other donor requests related to mailing lists (either print or digital) will be fully honored.
Internet Privacy Policy

What Information We Collect and How We Use That Information
The information that we collect relates directly to the action being completed. Depending on the action, we may collect your name, email address, email format preference (HTML vs. Text), address, interests, and similar information. For financial transactions such as donations or store purchases, we will need to collect your credit card number. We use customer information collected to send the requested information, complete the requested transaction, and present the most relevant information to our listeners based on their interests. We never sell, share or trade any of our customer information with other companies.
Any transaction that can be completed online may also be completed via phone or mail, should you have concerns about security. Please see our contact page for information on reaching our customer service team.

Truth For Life is the sole owner of the information collected on the website. This site does not collect personally identifying information about users except when you specifically and knowingly provide it. For example, we collect information from you in the following ways:

Security
All information provided to Truth For Life is transmitted using SSL (Secure Socket Layer) encryption. SSL is a proven coding system that lets your browser automatically encrypt, or scramble, data before you send it to us. We are compliant with the strictest industry standards for credit card security and processing, and our team regularly evaluates our systems and processes for additional safeguards.
Unfortunately, however, no data transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot ensure or warrant the security of such information. To increase the security of your transactions, please protect your usernames and passwords, be sure to log-out of the site when completing your transaction, and use a secure Internet connection when completing a financial transaction.

Shipping policy
Standard shipping to the United States
United States Postal Service is our standard in shipping method in the United States. Barring complications, your order will be processed and shipped from our fulfillment office in Cleveland, OH within 1-2 business days of purchase. You should expect to receive your order within 7-10 business days.
In Ohio: Shipping and handling fees for orders purchased or shipped in Ohio are subject to 6.5% sales tax
Expedited shipping within the United States
UPS is our primary method for expedited shipping within the United States. Please call 888.588.7884 for expedited shipping service, weekdays, 8:00 am – 5:00 pm (ET). Orders placed by 1:00 pm (ET) are guaranteed to arrive in two business days.
International Shipping
International orders will be shipped using United States Postal Service. Any duty charges assessed in shipment will be the responsibility of the purchaser. Your order will be processed and shipped from our fulfillment office in Cleveland, OH within 1-2 business days of being placed.
Please note: It is the responsibility of the person placing the order to provide a correct address, deliverable by USPS for standard shipments or UPS for expedited shipments. If given an incorrect address (no apartment number or building number) by purchaser and product is returned to Truth For Life, the purchaser may be responsible for the loss of the product as well as shipping and handling costs. Shipping cost is non-refundable for unclaimed, undeliverable, or returned packages, unless we made a mistake in processing.
Whistleblower Policy
Truth For Life is committed to operating in furtherance of its tax-exempt purposes and in compliance with all applicable laws, rules and regulations, including those concerning accounting and auditing, and prohibits fraudulent practices by any of its board members, officers, employees, or volunteers. This policy outlines a procedure for employees and volunteers to report actions that an employee or volunteer reasonably believes violates a law, or regulation or that constitutes fraudulent accounting or other practices. This policy applies to any matter which is related to Truth For Life's business and does not relate to private acts of an individual not connected to the business of Truth For Life.
If an employee or volunteer has a reasonable belief that an employee, volunteer or Truth For Life has engaged in any action that violates any applicable law, or regulation, including those concerning accounting and auditing, or constitutes a fraudulent practice, the employee or volunteer is expected to immediately report such information to Alistair Begg or Robert H. Butts. If the employee or volunteer does not feel comfortable reporting the information to either of the above, he or she is expected to report the information to any board member.
All reports will be followed up promptly, and an investigation conducted. In conducting its investigations, Truth For Life will strive to keep the identity of the complaining individual as confidential as possible, while conducting an adequate review and investigation.
Truth For Life will not retaliate against an employee or volunteer in the terms and conditions of employment because that employee or volunteer: (a) reports to a supervisor, to the executive director, the Board of Directors or to a federal, state or local agency what the employee believes in good faith to be a violation of the law; or (b) participates in good faith in any resulting investigation or proceeding, or (c) exercises his or her rights under any state or federal law(s) or regulation(s) to pursue a claim or take legal action to protect the employee's rights.
Truth For Life may take disciplinary action (up to and including termination) against an employee or volunteer who in management's assessment has engaged in retaliatory conduct in violation of this policy.
In addition, Truth For Life will not, with the intent to retaliate, take any action harmful to any employee or volunteer who has provided to law enforcement personnel or a court truthful information relating to the commission or possible commission by Truth For Life or any of its employees or volunteers of a violation of any applicable law or regulation.
Supervisors will be trained on this policy and Truth For Life's prohibition against retaliation in accordance with this policy.
Conflict of Interest Policy
Every member shall avoid conflicts of interest. Transactions with related parties may be undertaken only if all of the following are observed: 1) a material transaction is fully disclosed in the audited financial statements of the member; 2) the related party is excluded from the discussion and approval of such transaction; 3) a competitive bid or comparable valuation exists; and 4) the member's board has acted upon and demonstrated that the transaction is in the best interest of the member.
