What Kinds of Personal Information Do We Collect?
Personal Information
"Personal Information" refers to information through which you can be identified. We collect Personal Information that you provide voluntarily to us. Some examples of Personal Information that we may collect include your name, date of birth, postal address, e-mail address, telephone number, age, gender, and purchase information. If you apply for a job with us, you may be asked for other information, including your employment history. Some Personal Information is considered "Confidential Personal Information." Confidential Personal Information is your name in combination with one or more of the following: credit or debit card number, financial account number, e-mail address, street address, driver's license number, state identification card number, social security number, passport number, and naturalization number.
Non-Personal Information
"Non-Personal Information" refers to information about you or your activities through which you cannot be personally identified. When you use the Internet, your web browser or software may transmit certain information to the servers that host the websites you visit. Such information may include, for example, the unique number assigned to your server or Internet connection, your geographic location, and your interactions with the Site, including what pages you view and what information you download. When you visit the Site, your Non-Personal Information may be collected. Non-Personal Information may be linked to Personal Information.
Children’s Privacy
If you are under twenty-one (21) years old, do not send any Personal Information about yourself to this Site. In the event that we discover that a person under the age of twenty-one (21) has provided Personal Information, we will immediately delete the person’s information. If you are the parent or guardian of a person under 18 and believe that the person has provided us with Personal Information, you may email us at legal@flyingdogbrewery.com.

Information Collected Automatically:
We may collect information from you automatically using these technologies:
Cookies
As with many websites, the Site uses a technology called "cookies." A cookie is a collection of information that gets stored on the hard drive of your computer when browsing the Internet. These small bits of code automatically identify your browser to the Site whenever your computer is used to visit the Site. Cookies also let us know how many people visit the Site and where visitors go on the Site. In addition, cookies can let us know non-personally identifiable information, like how many times a user has been on certain pages. For registered users, cookies can make using the Site easier for you by saving information such as your passwords or preferences. By tracking how and when you use the Site, cookies help us determine which areas are popular and which are not. Many improvements and updates to the Site are based on data obtained from cookies. Accepting cookies allows you, among other things, to personalize your experience on the Site. Cookies may also allow the Site to present to you advertising that may be of interest to you. If you do not want information collected through the use of cookies, your browser should contain an option that allows you to disable or delete cookie data at any time. Some areas of the Site, however, may not provide you with a personalized experience if you have disabled the use of cookies. Some of our business partners (e.g., advertisers) use cookies on our Site. We have no access to or control over these cookies. This Privacy Notice covers the use of cookies by the Site only and does not cover the use of cookies by any advertisers.

When Do We Share Your Personal Information and Non-Personal Information?
We share your Personal Information and Non-Personal Information as is reasonable and necessary to accomplish the purposes described in this Privacy Notice. For example, if you order a mug from our Online Store, we share your Personal Information with the credit card company so that we will be paid for the mug and we share your contact information with the shipping company so that the mug can delivered to you.
Here are some other more specific examples of how we share information:
Online Store. Our online store uses an ecommerce platform provided by www.shopify.com to take orders and process credit card payments. Please see Shopify's privacy notice and terms of use for more information. We currently handle order fulfillment in-house.
Employment Applications. If you submit an employment application to us, you provide the information through ClearCompany’s website portal, and you should consult their privacy notice and terms of use concerning that information as well. Once we get the information from ClearCompany—which is immediately—we just use the information to pursue a potential employment relationship with you, and we get rid of it if you don’t come to work with us.
Contact Us Form Comments About Tap House at BWI. If you write a comment on our send us a message from our Contact Us tab on the Site, and indicate that you are contacting us about “BWI Tap House,” then in addition to routing the message to the top executives of Flying Dog, we also send the message to a company called HMS Host, which operates the BWI Tap House.
Email Communications. We use Mail Chimp to help us manage our email and marketing communications, so we share enough of your information with them to accomplish that purpose. Please see Mail Chimp’s privacy notice and terms of use for more information.
Event Organization. We sometimes gather information from people who are interested in events that we organize. We currently use www.eventbrite.com to do this, and you should see their privacy notice and terms of use for more information.
We do not currently disclose your Personal Information to third-party advertising agencies or advertiser. Some Non-Personal Information about you may be available to others through our use of Google Analytics or other similar analytical tools.
Browser “Do Not Track Signals”
Some web browsers may transmit "do not track" signals to the websites and other online services with which the browser communicates. There is no standard that governs what, if anything, websites should do when they receive these signals. FD currently does not take action in response to these signals. If and when a standard for responding is established, we may revisit our policy on responding to these signals.
