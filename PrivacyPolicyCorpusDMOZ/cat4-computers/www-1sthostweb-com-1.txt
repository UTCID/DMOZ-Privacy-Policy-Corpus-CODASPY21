What Personal Information
does 1stHostWeb collect?In
the course of serving its customers, 1stHostWeb acquires, stores and transmits
customer communications and information that customers may regard as private or
sensitive. Some of this information - such as the customer's name, address, telephone
number, and credit card data - is provided to 1stHostWeb by its customers in order
to establish service. Other information - such as the customer's account status,
choice of services, and customer logs - is created and maintained by1stHostWeb
in the normal course of providing service. 1stHostWeb also uses cookies, which
are small pieces of information that a web site can store in a designated file
on a user's computer for various reasons. In addition, 1stHostWeb may store customers'
electronic mail and other communications as a necessary incident to the transmission
and delivery of those communications.Data
Security1stHostWeb
will protect the confidentiality of its customers' information, account information
and personal communications to the fullest extent possible and consistent with
the law and the legitimate interests of 1stHostWeb, its partners, its employees
and other customers of 1stHostWeb’s services. To protect the loss, misuse,
and alteration of information that is collected from customers, 1stHostWeb has
appropriate physical, electronic, and managerial procedures in place.How
is customer information used?1stHostWeb
may share customer information with selected partners, for example, to provide
customers with information about products which might be of interest to the customer
or to enable the customer to take advantage of special partner programs. 1stHostWeb
may also use customer information to provide its customers with system information
or information about new or upgraded products.Modification
of customer information.Customers
may modify their personal information by notifying support@1stHostWeb.com of any
changes.Domain
name customers, who have registered a domain name with 1stHostWeb and wish to
change the Technical Contact from 1stHostWeb to another company or change any
other registration information, should be able to do so by Emailing support@1sthostweb.com
.1stHostWeb provides
all customers with full access to their domain account by sending them their own
username and password. It is important to note that customer domain name registration
information is made publicly available in the registry of domain names. In the
event you lose your Open SRS password, you can Email us and we will re-send the
password to the Email address on the domain registration record.Will
1stHostWeb ever send me direct mailings?From
time to time, 1stHostWeb may send its customers information about various products
and services that may be of interest. We will not send you information of this
type without specifically allowing you to "opt out" of such mailings.
You can opt out by sending an Email to support@1sthostweb.com .In
addition, if you are a 1stHostWeb reseller, 1stHostWeb and/or its partners and
advertisers may from time to time send you newsletters, promotional material,
and other mailings. You can opt out by sending an Email to support@1sthostweb.com
.How do I
protect my Personal Information in public forums?1stHostWeb
may offer interactive forums, such as message boards and chat. 1stHostWeb users
should be aware that when they voluntarily disclose Personal Information to other
users or to third parties on the 1stHostWeb system, through Email, or outside
of the 1stHostWeb system, that information can be collected and used by others
and may result in unsolicited messages from other members or third parties. While
1stHostWeb strives to protect your Personal Information, 1stHostWeb cannot guarantee
the security of any information that you transmit to 1stHostWeb, and you are solely
responsible for maintaining the secrecy of any passwords or other account information.What
about privacy on sites that 1stHostWeb links to?1stHostWeb
may provide links to its partners and other Internet sites. These sites have separate
data and privacy practices independent of 1stHostWeb, and 1stHostWeb disclaims
any responsibility or liability for their policies or actions.Disclosure
of customer information and communications1stHostWeb
will not otherwise disclose its customers' personal and account information unless
1stHostWeb has reason to believe that disclosing such information is necessary
to identify, make contact with, or bring legal action against someone who may
be causing harm or interfering with the rights or property of 1stHostWeb, 1stHostWeb’s
customers, or others, or where 1stHostWeb has a good faith belief that the law
requires such disclosure.1stHostWeb
also will not, except for reasons stated below, disclose to third parties the
contents of any electronic mail or other electronic communications that 1stHostWeb
stores or transmits for its customers. The circumstances under which 1stHostWeb
will disclose such electronic customer communications are when:
it
is necessary in order to provide service to the customer;
it
is necessary to protect the legitimate interests of 1stHostWeb and its customers;
it
is required to cooperate with interception orders, warrants, or other legal process
that 1stHostWeb determines in its sole discretion to be valid and enforceable;
and
it
is necessary to provide to a law enforcement agency when the contents are inadvertently
obtained by 1stHostWeb and appear to pertain to the commission of a crime.
1stHostWeb disclaims
any intention to censor, edit or engage in ongoing review or surveillance of communications
stored on or transmitted through its facilities by customers or others. 1stHostWeb
will, however, review, delete or block access to communications that may harm
1stHostWeb, its customers or third parties. The grounds on which 1stHostWeb may
take such action include, but are not limited to, actual or potential violations
of 1stHostWeb’s Acceptable Use Policy.
