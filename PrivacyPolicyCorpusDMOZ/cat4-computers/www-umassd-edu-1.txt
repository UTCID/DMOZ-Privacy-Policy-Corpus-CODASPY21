Privacy Statement
UMass Dartmouth respects the privacy of visitors to its website to the extent permitted by United States, state and federal laws. This privacy statement informs you of what information is collected by this website and webpages with the domain "www.umassd.edu" and how the information is used. Other University of Massachusetts websites may adopt different privacy statements or policies.
Your use of websites is subject to the privacy statement of the providers of those websites. You are encouraged to be aware when you leave this website to read the privacy statement on each website you visit. You can tell you are leaving this website by looking at the URL of the destination site.
When you access the UMass Dartmouth home page "www.umassd.edu", and all pages within the website, your visit is not tracked by the University using your individual name or any other personal information. However, this website does utilize Google AdWords, Facebook, LinkedIn, and other software that may track and collect personal information from your device from the time you access our website. The information automatically collected is subject to their privacy policies.
Some computer information and automatic and optional information is collected by third party vendors, such as Google AdWords and Facebook when you access the website. This information is subject to the privacy policies of these services. Information collected may include: Internet domain and internet address of the computer, mobile device or other device you use to access the website.
Essential technical information collected includes the identification of the page or service you are requesting, type of browser and operating system you are using; and the date and time of access.
Optional information is collected only if you choose to send it. For example, if you choose to complete an online form, register for an event, or send an email. Optional information that is collected may include: Your name, email address, and the content of your email.
EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
This website is intended for general use and is viewable on the internet throughout the world. If you are visiting this website from the European Union (EU), personal information you provide to the University may not be subject to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The GDPR replaces the Data Protection Directive 95/46/EC and was designed to strengthen data privacy protections for EU residents. Should you have additional questions or need information regarding GDPR’s impact and its application to personal information provided to the University, visit the GDPR website.

Cookies and Web Beacons
The website utilizes "cookies" to enhance your experience on this website and for marketing purposes. Cookies are text files we place in your computer's, mobile device’s, tablet’s or other device’s browser to help us improve our access to our website and identify repeat visitors to our website. Cookies can also enable us to track and target the interests of visitors to our website to enhance the experience on our website. We use cookies to understand site usage and to improve the content and offerings on our website. We also may use cookies to offer you products, programs, or services, or to provide you with personalized online display advertising tailored to your interests. In addition, we may use the services of third parties to collect and use anonymous information about your visits to and interactions with our website through the use of technologies such as cookies to personalize advertisements. Cookies do not store personal information about you, unless you voluntarily provide it. UMass Dartmouth does not collect any personal information about you unless you provide that information voluntarily by sending an e-mail, completing an UMass Dartmouth Information Request Form, or completing an online application.
You may refuse cookies by turning them off in your browser. Your browser may also be set not to accept cookies.
UMass Dartmouth may collect a limited set of non-personal information about you to help manage our online advertising and promotions, we or our third party advertising providers use "web beacons" (also known as "clear gifs" and "pixel tags"), in conjunction with cookies as non-personal information. Web beacons are small transparent graphic images used by many websites in connection with cookies. For more information about the use of this technology by us and our third-party advertising service providers, including how to opt out of these technologies, please visit the Consumer Opt Out page on Network Advertising Initiative and Adobe Digital Marketing Suite Opt Out page.
UMass Dartmouth may use remarketing pixels created by Google AdWords, DoubleClick, LinkedIn, Facebook, or other services to show you ads or relevant content on these channels or across the Internet. These marketing pixels collect information about the websites you visit, which are stored by these services and then provides you ads on other websites.
