Your information is safe with us - this is our Privacy Policy

What personal data we collect, why we collect it, and how we use it:
Information you voluntarily submit to the website. We may collect personal information from you such as your name or email address.  For example, you may voluntarily submit information to the website by requesting a trial, placing an order, subscribing to a newsletter, or submitting a contact form.  In addition, we may ask you to create a user profile, which would allow you to create a username and password.  We will store your name, the username that you create, but your password is not visible in our records.
Information we collect from others. We may receive information about you from other sources.  For example, if you use a third-party software such as Authorize.net (when you purchase software), they will transfer information to us which we will use for order fulfillment.
Automatically collected information. We automatically collect certain information about you and the device you use to access this website.  For example, when you visit this website, we will log your IP address, operating system, browser used, referring website, the pages you viewed, and the date/time that you visited.  We may also collect information about the actions you take when using the website, such as the links you clicked.
Cookies: We may log information using cookies, which are small data files stored in your browser by the website.  We use both session cookies (which expire when you close your browser) and persistent cookies (which stay in your browser until you delete them) to provide you with a more personal experience on the website.

To respond to your comments or inquiries.
To provide you with user support.
To track and measure advertising on the website.
To process payment for purchases you make through the website via Authorize.Net.
To protect, investigate, and deter against unauthorized or illegal activity.

Occasionally we may use anonymous data, which does not identify you specifically, or when combined with data from other parties.  This type of anonymous data may be provided to other parties for marketing, advertising, or other uses.  Examples of anonymous data may include analytics or information from cookies.
