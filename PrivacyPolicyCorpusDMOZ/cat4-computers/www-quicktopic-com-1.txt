IP Address
The IP Address from which you post a message (via the web form
or via email), if available, is made available to be visible to the
topic administrator, for the purpose of allowing the
topic administrator to disallow
further posts from that IP Address at the topic administrator's
discretion. (The topic administrator is by default the person
who created the discussion thread, but administration of a topic
can be transferred to or shared with other users.)
The said IP Address can potentially be used to identify the
computer from which you posted a message.
Contacting topic administrator by email
A facility is provided for
contacting the topic administrator by email, using a web-based
form linked at the bottom of the topic page via the link
with the text "Contact Us". The topic administrator's email
address is not revealed to readers, but readers are able to
send email to the topic administrator using this form. In
order to reply to email received via this facility, the
topic administrator can use his or her own email facilities;
Quick Topic does not provide a reply facility.
Cookies
Cookies are files that are sent from our site to your browser,
which your browser can then store on your disk.
On subsequent visits to the same site, your browser can send these
files back to us. They're generally
used to store information between visits to a specific site.
If you register and sign in to QuickTopic, QuickTopic uses cookies
to store your session ID which maintains your signed-in session.
If you opt to receive email for posts to topics, QuickTopic stores
your email address in a cookie so that you can easily opt out of
this by unsubscribing.
Third party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to your website or other websites.
Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to your users based on their visit to your sites and/or other sites on the Internet.
